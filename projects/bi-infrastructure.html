The Problem

Bank SinoPac's analytics team had no governance on Tableau Server. Finding the right report often took longer than building a new one — knowledge lived in people's heads, not in a system.

  • 20+ analysts across departments, no shared catalog
  • 300+ reports on Tableau Server with no naming convention or ownership tracking
  • Reports inconsistently named, ownership unclear
  • Duplicate dashboards proliferating across teams

Part 1 — 300-Dimension Customer Profile Dashboard

A full-bank profiling dashboard built for three distinct use cases — all without filing an analyst request.

  • Segment profiling — quickly surface the demographic and behavioral fingerprint of any customer group
  • Latent value discovery — identify high-potential customers not yet engaged on key products
  • Exploratory analysis — investigate specific transaction patterns on the fly

Key design decisions that made it work:

  • 300+ filter dimensions hidden in a hamburger menu — demographics, products, transactions, channels, credit history
  • Parameter-driven comparison — every selection auto-compares against the unselected base, so every number reads as a direct delta
  • Drill-down KPIs — Wealth AP expands into ETF / US equities / bonds / structured products; no new view needed

Dashboard in Action — Downstream Analysis

The dashboard became a launchpad for deeper business analysis. Example below: salary-credit customer segmentation, comparing product breadth and asset depth across tiers.

  • High-value payroll customers use more products but allocate less depth per product — cross-sell signal
  • Standard payroll customers concentrate assets in savings — wealth penetration opportunity
  • Non-payroll customers show 87% fund concentration among wealth holders — product preference clearly segmented
  • Insights fed directly into product strategy and marketing targeting decisions

Part 2 — Tableau Server Data Catalog

An automated sitemap tool that crawled the Server, extracted workbook metadata, and surfaced everything through a filterable catalog — click a workbook name to open it directly.

  • 5 filter dimensions — segment, platform, product, workbook name, updated month
  • Keyword sidebar with 10+ common quick-filters for the most frequent search patterns
  • Hot workbooks panel — top 5 most-viewed reports in the last 30 days, for new analysts to orient fast
  • View count heatmap — right-edge heat bars surface stale workbooks and governance gaps at a glance
  • 50% faster report search time
  • 75%+ monthly adoption across the analyst org

What I Learned

BI infrastructure work is invisible until it breaks — or until it suddenly works. The highest-leverage thing I built wasn't any single chart; it was the governance layer that made all future dashboards easier to build, find, and trust.

  • The parameter comparison design came from watching analysts manually duplicate filters — once you see the pattern, the solution is obvious
  • Making data self-serve reduces analyst queue depth more durably than hiring more analysts
