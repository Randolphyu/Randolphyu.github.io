The Problem

Bank SinoPac's analytics team had grown to over 200 analysts across departments — but the Tableau Server that housed all their reports had no governance. Reports were named inconsistently, ownership was unclear, and finding the right dashboard could take longer than building a new one. Duplicate reports proliferated. Institutional knowledge lived in people's heads.

Data Catalog & Sitemap Tool

I took on the role of Tableau Server administrator and BI consultant, and the first priority was discoverability. I built an automated sitemap tool that crawled the server, extracted metadata, and generated a structured catalog — organized by business domain, owner, and update frequency.

The result: report search time dropped by 50%, and monthly active usage of the Server climbed to 75%+ of the analyst org. People could finally find what they needed without asking a colleague.

300-Dimension Customer Profile Dashboard

The second project was more ambitious. Product managers and analysts were spending ~40 hours every month manually pulling and cross-referencing customer data to answer questions like: "Who are our high-value customers in this segment, and what products do they hold?"

I designed a customer profile dashboard with 300+ dimensions — spanning demographics, transaction behavior, product holdings, credit history, and channel preferences. The data model required orchestrating ETL pipelines across multiple source systems and building a dimensional model that could support arbitrary filtering without query rewrites.

The dashboard shipped as a self-serve tool for PMs. The 40-hour monthly task became a few clicks.

What I Learned

BI infrastructure work is invisible until it breaks — or until it suddenly works. The highest-leverage thing I did wasn't any single dashboard; it was establishing the governance layer that made all future dashboards easier to build, find, and trust.
