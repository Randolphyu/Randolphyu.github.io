Push Notification CTR — 90% Lift (Gamania)

At Gamania, marketing push notifications were underperforming. Rather than guessing at the cause, I designed a multivariate A/B testing framework using stratified sampling across 500K+ users — testing message copy, send timing, and segmentation criteria simultaneously.

Statistical analysis using chi-square and t-tests identified the winning combination. CTR increased by 90%. More importantly, the framework itself became reusable infrastructure for future campaigns.

Installment Conversion — 50% Lift (Bank SinoPac)

Weekly installment conversion was a priority metric for the digital banking product team. I ran funnel analysis to identify where users were dropping off — and found a clear drop-off window 2–3 days after initial product exposure.

Targeted push notifications sent within that window, personalized by transaction history, boosted conversion by 50%. The insight was in the timing, not just the message.

FX Transaction Growth — 30% YoY (Bank SinoPac)

Using RFM segmentation (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) and association rule analysis on asset and transaction data, I identified customer segments most likely to respond to FX product recommendations and the bundled product combinations most likely to convert.

The segmentation model fed personalized product recommendation campaigns that drove 30% year-over-year FX transaction growth.

On Experimentation in Practice

The hardest part of A/B testing isn't the statistics — it's getting stakeholders to wait for significance before shipping. Building trust in the process, and communicating results clearly to non-technical PMs, was as important as the analysis itself.
